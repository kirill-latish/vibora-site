Shot Guide
Reference guide for all padel shot types and their IMU/mic detection signatures.
Offensive Overheads
Smash
Remate
▼
The most powerful shot in padel. Flat overhead with maximum acceleration, hit before the ball bounces off the back wall.
Very high peak magnitude (4–6g), sharp downward arc, minimal wrist snap.
Bajada
▼
Overhead shot played after the ball bounces off the back wall, hit as it descends. Lower contact point than smash, more controlled.
Moderate magnitude, slower arc than smash, more follow-through rotation.
Bandeja
▼
Controlled overhead with sidespin, used to maintain net position instead of going for a winner. Slice motion with horizontal arm path.
Moderate magnitude, significant lateral rotation, characteristic cross-body follow-through.
Vibora
▼
Overhead with extreme wrist supination and topspin. The defining shot of modern padel.
High rotation rate on z-axis at contact, wrist snaps over the ball, distinct follow-through arc.
Rulo
▼
Similar to vibora but with opposite spin direction — the wrist pronates rather than supinates. Produces a different bounce direction.
Very similar magnitude to vibora but rotation rate inverts on z-axis.
Net Shots (Volleys)
Forehand Volley
Volea de derecha
▼
Short compact punch at net. Quick wrist extension.
Low magnitude, short window, forehand rotation on z-axis.
Backhand Volley
Volea de revés
▼
Same compact punch but backhand. Slightly different elbow/shoulder angle creates a distinct attitude signature.
Mirror of forehand volley, rotation inverts on z-axis.
Block Volley
Volea de bloqueo
▼
No swing — racket is held firm and ball is deflected. Very low peak magnitude.
Almost no acceleration spike from swing. Impact creates sharp vibration.
Groundstrokes
Forehand
Derecha
▼
Baseline forehand, hit after bounce. Full arm swing with follow-through.
Medium magnitude, clear forehand rotation, longer window than volley.
Backhand
Revés
▼
Baseline backhand. Two-handed and one-handed versions have different IMU signatures.
Two-handed produces more symmetric acceleration profile.
Forehand Slice
Slice de derecha
▼
Underspin forehand, cut motion. Wrist opens through the ball rather than closing.
Downward brush at contact, open wrist path.
Backhand Slice
Slice de revés
▼
The most common defensive groundstroke in padel. Low contact, open racket face, cut motion.
Distinct downward-then-outward wrist path.
Lobs
Defensive Lob
Globo defensivo
▼
Hit from back of court under pressure. Slow, high arc, maximum height.
Low magnitude, upward wrist trajectory, long gentle follow-through.
Offensive Lob
Globo ofensivo
▼
Disguised as a groundstroke but with sudden upward redirect at contact.
Preparation looks like forehand/backhand, characteristic upward flick at impact.
Serves
Topspin Serve
Saque liftado
▼
The standard padel serve hit with upward brush.
Upward wrist snap at contact, moderate magnitude, distinctive preparation shoulder rotation.
Slice Serve
Saque cortado
▼
Serve with sidespin, cuts away after bounce.
Lateral wrist cut at contact, racket face opens outward.
Power Serve
Saque potente
▼
Flat fast serve, maximum pace.
Highest magnitude of all serves, minimal wrist snap, more arm-driven.
Kick Serve
Saque con efecto
▼
Heavy topspin serve that bounces high. Strong upward wrist brush, pronounced shoulder rotation.
Similar to topspin serve but higher rotation rate.
Wall Shots
Forehand off Back Wall
Contra pared de derecha
▼
Ball played after bouncing from back wall. Player moves backward, contacts rising ball.
Backward body momentum creates different gravity vector in attitude data.
Backhand off Back Wall
Contra pared de revés
▼
Mirror version. Same characteristic backward movement signature.
Mirror of forehand off back wall.
Off Two Walls
Doble pared
▼
Ball played after hitting back wall then side wall or vice versa. Player often has to improvise.
Highly variable — the hardest class to train reliably.
Soft / Touch Shots
Chiquita
▼
Soft low shot played at the feet of net players, forcing them to volley upward. Short compact motion, very low magnitude.
Similar to block volley but with a small forward push rather than pure deflection.
Drop Shot
Dejada
▼
Soft shot just over the net with heavy underspin, lands very short.
Low magnitude, strong downward brush at contact, minimal follow-through.
Lob Chiquita
Globo chiquita
▼
A chiquita that unexpectedly converts to a lob. Preparation looks like chiquita, sudden upward flick at contact. Very deceptive.
IMU captures the upward redirect clearly.
Defensive Specialty
The Snake
La serpiente
▼
Extreme defensive shot, played very low and hard cross-court off the back wall. Aggressive full-swing groundstroke from a difficult position.
High magnitude but unusual body attitude due to the low contact point.
Spinning Shot
Rueda
▼
Player rotates fully through the shot, sometimes used when ball is tight to the side wall.
Unmistakable multi-second acceleration pattern before contact from full body rotation.
